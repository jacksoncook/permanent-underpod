## 0:00–5:00 — Cold Open + Intros

**Hook line:** *"Korean traders just swapped Bitcoin for AI stocks, eight banks are racing to launch a won stablecoin, and the most interesting new payments customer might be an AI with a wallet. We picked this week to start a podcast."*

- 75–90 seconds each: name, what you do, and **the first crypto you ever bought and at what price** (humanizes everyone, seeds the mistakes segment).
- One sentence on the show's premise: two people who disagree about whose money wins — won, dollar, or neither.
- **Own the empty chair on air.** Don't hide it: *"There was supposed to be a Bitcoin maxi in the third seat — he bailed. We'll argue his side badly tonight and let him correct us in episode 2."* A last-minute cancellation is great audio if you lean into it; it's awkward only if you pretend it didn't happen.
- **Tip:** Record the cold open LAST, after the outro, when everyone is warm. Cut it to the front in the edit.

## 5:00–14:00 — The News: What the Shakeout Revealed (and Where the Money Went)

Structuring question both hosts must answer and commit to: **is this a crypto problem, or is crypto losing the attention war to AI?**

**Two-host split:** stablecoin host leads the flows/macro beats; Korea host leads the rotation beats. Neither needs Lightning depth here — this segment is unaffected by the cancellation.

**Price context in ONE sentence, then move on** (we're not a charts show): BTC is down roughly half from its October peak after a brutal two-week flush — the story isn't the number, it's what the flush exposed.

**The flows and the structure (this is the meat):**
- Spot BTC ETFs: **$3.4B weekly outflow — worst week since launch**. IBIT's worst week ever ($980M out). First real stress test of the "institutions are here forever" thesis.
- June 4–5: **~$3B liquidated**, 127,000+ traders wiped in one 24h window — a leverage story, not a value story. Who was actually positioned, and how?
- Macro in one breath: April CPI hot, May jobs hot, June Fed-cut odds collapsed 32% → 8%. Fed on hold at 3.5–3.75%.

**The AI rotation (the angle most shows are missing — set up the Fable thread here):**
- Korean retail didn't leave the market — it rotated into AI: crypto volume is now **2% of KOSPI turnover**; in July 2025 crypto *exceeded* equities. Samsung's market cap is approaching Bitcoin's.
- Zoom out: the AI boom — Anthropic's new Claude Fable 5, the frontier-model arms race — is pulling the speculative oxygen that used to belong to crypto. (Talking points in the Claude Fable / AI section below.)
- Commit on air: is the rotation to AI **cyclical or structural**?

**Nuance to land on air — the Strategy "sale":**
- Strategy sold **32 BTC for $2.5M** (May 26–31) — **0.0038%** of its 843,706 BTC stack — to pay contractually required STRC preferred dividends. Saylor pre-announced this a year ago. The market panicking over $2.5M is itself the story about sentiment.

**Also in the mix:** Mt. Gox moved **10,422 BTC ($739M)** on June 2; ~34,500 BTC remain, repayment deadline **Oct 31, 2026**.

**Korea read (hand to Korea host):** Bitcoin trades at a kimchi **discount** of ~-3% — Korean retail is telling a different story than US ETF flows (full story in the Korea segment).

## 24:00–36:00 — Main Event: Stablecoins vs the Banks (and the Bitcoin Rail Question)

The week handed the two of you a perfect two-hander — the incumbents stormed in: **June 3** — Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, Coinbase reportedly backing a joint stablecoin platform. **June 5** — JPMorgan, Citi, BofA, Wells Fargo building a shared tokenized-deposit network to compete with stablecoins (target H1 2027). Central question: **do stablecoins eat the banks, or do the banks absorb stablecoins?**

**Stablecoin corner (stablecoin host leads; Korea host prosecutes):**
- Total market cap **~$311–325B**. USDT ~$186–190B, USDC ~$77B (together ~93%).
- Tether Q1 profit: **$1.04B**; holds **~$141B in US Treasuries — 17th-largest holder on Earth**; 570M users; first-ever full KPMG audit underway.
- Honest-numbers grenade: the famous "$33T stablecoin volume" is mostly bots/rebalancing. McKinsey/Artemis put **real payment volume at ~$390B annualized**. Myth-bust it.

**The bank counterattack (Korea host argues the banks' side — Korea is literally running the banks-first model):**
- One plain-English sentence on tokenized deposits vs stablecoins, then: if the banks' network lands H1 2027, is that the banks conceding the stablecoin thesis was right — or smothering it?
- GENIUS Act final rules due July 18 — whoever the rules favor wins the US round.

**The Bitcoin rail question — steelman in absentia (~4 min, hedged):**
- This was the missing host's corner. **Say it once on air — "we'll give the Bitcoin side its fair shot and let the expert correct us in episode 2" — then use the prepared numbers and don't improvise protocol depth.**
- The synthesis fact that makes it discussable without him: **USDT went live ON Lightning (March 21, 2026, via Taproot Assets)** — so "stablecoins vs Bitcoin rails" is a false binary; the live question is whether real volume routes that way.
- Prepared numbers (read, don't riff): Lightning volume **4x'd in 2025 to $1.17B/month** (River) even as public capacity *declined*; **15% of Coinbase BTC withdrawals** go over Lightning; Square turned it on by default for **~4M US merchants** (March 2026), fee-free through 2026. Trackers disagree wildly on network size (~4,800–5,700 BTC) — "we can't even agree how big the network is" still works as a beat.
- Honest headwinds (easier to voice with no Lightning loyalist in the room): David Marcus (Lightspark CEO): self-custodial Lightning "really doesn't work well"; **Ark's first mainnet implementation (Bark) launched June 9**; El Salvador's mandatory-acceptance era is over (IMF deal). **If a question goes deeper than these bullets, park it explicitly for episode 2 — that's the cliffhanger, not a weakness.**

**The wildcard customer — AI agents (give this ~3 min, it's the freshest angle):**
- The winner of payments may not be serving humans at all. AI agents are becoming economic actors: they can't pass bank KYC, but they **can** hold a wallet.
- Make it concrete with Claude Fable 5: Anthropic's new top-tier model meters work at **$10 in / $50 out per million tokens**. When one agent buys thirty seconds of another agent's work, that's a half-cent invoice — the exact payment shape card rails are worst at and crypto rails were built for.
- Put the question to both hosts: *which rail does an agent actually use to pay a half-cent invoice — a stablecoin, Lightning, or neither?*
- Honesty check (say it on air): agentic-payments volume today is tiny — the fair framing is "the most credible new demand story for crypto rails since remittances," not a present-tense fact.

**Hand-off out of the segment:** *"And there's exactly one country running this experiment at national scale, right now, with real deadlines…"* → Korea deep-dive.

## 36:00–45:00 — Korea Deep-Dive: The Great Rotation + the KRW Stablecoin Race

Korea host leads; stablecoin host plays the skeptical foreigner. This was "material to weave in" with three hosts — with two, it's a full segment and your Korea host carries it.

**The Great Rotation (lead story):**
- Bitcoin at a kimchi **discount** of **-2.8% to -3.6%** in early June — deepest since 2021. (The 9.7% *premium* spike was Feb 2025 — old news; don't cite it as current.)
- Korean retail rotated into KOSPI AI/semiconductor stocks: daily crypto volume across all five exchanges (~₩2.7T) is now **2% of KOSPI turnover (₩118T)**. In July 2025, crypto *exceeded* equities. Samsung's market cap (~$1.36T) is approaching Bitcoin's (~$1.47T).
- Korea's most-traded asset is now **Worldcoin — ahead of XRP**. Peak Korea.

**KRW stablecoin race:**
- DABA shelved until after June 3 local elections — **Lee's party won**, fight resumes in July. Core deadlock: BOK demands banks own ≥51% of issuers; FSC resists. FSC proposal: issuers need **100%+ reserves**.
- Eight-bank KRW stablecoin consortium. USDT/USDC **excluded** from Korea's approved corporate investment list — the won-vs-dollar stablecoin fight is the live one.
- Banks aren't waiting: **Hana Bank's $670M purchase of 6.55% of Dunamu closes June 15 — four days after this recording.**
- Naver–Dunamu $10.3B merger: delayed by FSS/KFTC; shareholder vote Aug 18, close Sept 30.
- **The digital won is NOT paused:** Project Hangang Phase 2 launched March 18 with nine banks and real subsidy payments. New BOK Governor Shin Hyun-song's inaugural address pushed CBDC + bank deposit tokens and pointedly omitted stablecoins.

**Comedy gold:** In February, **Bithumb accidentally credited users 620,000 BTC (~$56B) instead of ₩620,000 (~$450) each** during a promo. Flash crash, frozen accounts, 99.7% recovered — and a new national rule: exchanges must reconcile balances **every 5 minutes** (was every 24 hours).

**Closing beat:** *does anyone in Seoul actually use Lightning?* The honest answer is good audio either way — and it tees up the absent expert for episode 2.

## Claude Fable / AI × Crypto Material (woven into News + Main Event)

**What Claude Fable actually is (so nobody fumbles it on air):**
- Anthropic's newest frontier model, **Claude Fable 5** — a new tier *above* the existing Opus/Sonnet/Haiku lineup. One plain-English sentence per the jargon rule: *"model tiers are like seat classes on a plane — Fable is the new first class."*
- Pricing: **$10 per million input tokens / $50 per million output tokens** (Opus 4.8 is $5/$25, Sonnet 4.6 is $3/$15, Haiku 4.5 is $1/$5). Context window: 1M tokens — roughly a few thousand pages in one sitting.

**Why it belongs on a crypto payments show (the bridge, not a tech demo):**
- AI work is **token-metered** — billed per unit of machine labor. That makes machine-to-machine payments tiny and constant: half-cent invoices, thousands of times a day. Cards can't clear that economically; Lightning and stablecoins were designed for exactly it.
- Agents can't open bank accounts (no KYC for software), but they can hold wallets. Whoever wins the agent-wallet default wins a customer base that never sleeps and never churns.
- The rotation thread: the same AI boom Fable headlines is what Korean retail rotated *into* — AI is simultaneously crypto's biggest capital competitor and its most credible new use case. That tension is the segment.

**Guardrails:**
- Don't overclaim — agent-payments volume today is negligible; frame it as the demand story to watch, not a current stat.
- Optional meta-beat for the outro: *"Full disclosure — an AI helped build tonight's prep doc. The irony is not lost on us."*

## First-Episode Survival Notes

1. **Record 75–80 min, edit to 60.** Best material comes after minute 20.
2. **Appoint one clock owner** with explicit permission to interrupt.
3. **One-sentence jargon rule** — first mention of Lightning / reserves / kimchi premium / AI model tiers (Claude Fable) gets one plain-English sentence, then move on.
4. **Record the cold-open hook last.**
5. **Prep tonight:** mistake numbers written down; **two** contrarian takes each; the Korea host skims the stablecoin expert's blog post; both review the Lightning "steelman in absentia" bullets until comfortable reading them cold.
6. **We are not a price show** — one sentence of price context per segment max; if the conversation drifts into chart talk, the clock owner pulls it back to flows, builders, and policy.
7. **Hedge second-tier stats:** anything from a single smaller outlet (e.g., the $3.4B ETF week breakdown), say "reportedly."
8. **Don't bluff Bitcoin depth.** You know enough to be dangerous, not enough to win a protocol argument. Acknowledge the missing expert once, lean on the prepared numbers, and park anything deeper for episode 2 — out loud, as a tease. Confidence about your limits reads better than faked expertise.
9. **Two voices = faster dead air.** Agreement kills a two-hander; if you both agree, one of you argues the absent Bitcoiner's position. Keep the Pocket Topics list printed and in front of you.
