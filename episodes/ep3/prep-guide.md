## 6:00–24:00 — MAIN EVENT: The 100x Trade (explain it, then execute live)

Ep 2 was the perps *theory*; Ep 3 is the *lab*. Chris re-frames in one or two sentences (jargon rule — we already explained it), then **we each open a real, tiny, high-leverage position on camera** and narrate it.

**The 60-second re-frame (Chris):**
- A perp = a leveraged bet on price with **no expiry**; the **funding rate** (a periodic long↔short payment) tethers it to spot. At **100x, your margin is ~1% of notional — so a ~1% move against you = liquidation.** (Callback: that's why James Wynn keeps blowing up — see News.)

**The reality check (this is the segment's spine — the legal version is boring, the fun version is offshore):**
- **Onshore/regulated US perps now EXIST but are low-leverage:** the CFTC approved **Kalshi's BTCPERP on May 29, 2026** (first US-regulated perp); **Coinbase** US perps (since Jul 21, 2025) cap at **~10x**; **Kraken** launched CFTC-regulated US perps **June 15, 2026** (on Bitnomial, 8-hour funding). *(verify caps day-of.)*
- **100x is still an offshore game** (Binance/Bybit/OKX advertise up to ~125x — directional, varies by tier/region). So the honest tension: *"the version Washington blessed caps you at 10x; to actually do the dumb thing we have to leave the country (digitally)."* Discuss whether that gap protects retail or just exports the casino.

**Execute live (the format):**
1. Each host puts a **fixed tiny stake** (suggest **$50–$100 real, or stated paper if anyone's not comfortable — say which on air**) into a **100x** BTC (or alt) position. State direction + thesis in one sentence.
2. On screen for each: **entry, leverage, liquidation price, and the % move that wipes it.** Pull a **live funding-rate reading** (CoinGlass) so viewers see the toll.
3. **Friendly bracket:** last one un-liquidated wins; loser does [forfeit]. Leave the positions open and **cut back to the P&L during News and at the Outro.**
4. The self-aware button: *"we are doing, live, the exact thing that turned Jared into a cautionary tale."*

**Talking points while the trades breathe:**
- Funding mechanics: Hyperliquid pays funding **hourly** (capped 4%/hr); CEXs typically 8h. Holding a crowded long is a constant bleed even if you're right on direction.
- **Hyperliquid scale** as the backdrop: record **~6.9% of all perp open interest (May 24, 2026)**, **70%+ of DEX perp activity**, ~**$9.6B OI**; permissionless **HIP-3** markets (oil, gold, S&P 500, pre-IPO names) ~**$1.4B+ OI** — *caveat on air:* one report says **>90% of HIP-3 sits with a single deployer**, so "decentralized price discovery" is doing a lot of work.
- The thesis tie-in: scratchers → perps → prediction markets, "the same retail impulse with more leverage and better UX." Cash the still-owed **Australia gambling-rules** beat here if Chris/Tyler want it.

## 48:00–59:00 — STRC / MSTR ("remember when we said *don't buy Stretch*?")

The callback segment. Ep 2: *"you could just be getting 11 in stretch… do not buy stretch. This is not financial advice."* Ep 3: Stretch just hit a **record low** and there's a live **"death spiral"** debate. Pay it off.

**What STRC actually is (jargon rule — one clean pass):**
- **STRC "Stretch"** = Strategy's **variable-rate perpetual preferred stock**, sold to retail as a low-volatility, monthly-pay **yield product engineered to trade near $100 par**. IPO'd **July 2025** (~**$2.52B** raised, bought ~21,021 BTC). The dividend **resets monthly** via a VWAP framework to pin the price near $100 — drifts below par, they **raise the rate**; above, they cut it.
- **It started at 9%; it's now 11.50%** (held four-plus months; raised ~7× since launch) — *say 11.5%, not 12%.* On **June 18, 2026 STRC hit a record low ~$89**, pushing the effective yield to **~12.9%**. It just switched to **semi-monthly** payments (effective June 30).
- The family, one line each: **STRK** "Strike" (~8%, the convertible one), **STRF** "Strife" (10% fixed, most senior), **STRD** "Stride" (highest yield, **non-cumulative** — skip it and it's gone), **STRC** "Stretch" (variable, that's this one).

**The number that matters — mNAV:**
- MSTR now trades at a **DISCOUNT to its own Bitcoin: ~0.72–0.85x NAV** (down from **~1.16x in March 2026**, ~1.26x last September). *(Phrase as a range on air; methodologies differ. EV-based ~1.1x — don't conflate.)*
- **Why it's the whole ballgame:** above mNAV 1, issuing stock to buy BTC is *accretive* (raises BTC-per-share) — the "infinite money glitch." **Below 1, it reverses:** every share sold to buy BTC *shrinks* BTC-per-share. The engine is now running backwards.

**The receipt (and the Ep 2 callback):**
- Strategy holds **~847,000 BTC** at an **average cost ~$75,650** — i.e., **underwater at ~$64K**.
- **They sold Bitcoin.** Late May 2026, Strategy **sold 32 BTC (~$2.5M, ~$77,135 avg)** — **first sale since 2022** — and the **8-K literally says proceeds are "expected to fund distributions on preferred stock."** Saylor's framing: *"we'll probably sell some bitcoin to pay a dividend just to inoculate the market."* They're now selling the collateral to pay the yield.
- **Cash-flow mismatch:** **>$1.2B/yr** in preferred dividends against a legacy software business doing **~$477M/yr** — funded by capital markets, not operations. Works only while they can keep issuing.

**The debate:**
- **Bear / "death spiral" (Peter Schiff, and Bloomberg reportedly says retire STRC):** raise the yield → must sell more MSTR at bigger discounts to fund it; don't raise it → STRC keeps falling; cancel the dividend → STRC crashes and takes MSTR + BTC with it. Selling BTC erodes the collateral; below-par ATM is dilutive.
- **Bull rebuttal (Benchmark, TD Cowen):** the death-spiral math doesn't hold; a little BTC sold to pay dividends *"makes the credit more creditworthy"*; needs BTC back to **~$90–100K** to re-premium and resume issuing at par.
- **Sector context:** this isn't just MSTR — **~40% of the top-100 BTC treasury companies trade below NAV**, ~**$10B** combined unrealized losses. **Metaplanet** ~35,102 BTC, ~**$1.45B** underwater (mNAV 8x→~1.3x); **Nakamoto (NAKA)** the clearest blowup (**mNAV 75x → ~0.7x, −96%**).
- **The show's angle:** is STRC a clever retail yield product or **rent-seeking on yield-chasers** (callback to Ep 2's rent-seeking bit)? And is the whole treasury-company model the **gambling-on-leverage thesis in a suit** — same impulse as our 100x trades, just institutional?
